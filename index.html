The Idea

the three-beat story

Modest by Design
the silicon

A PowerPC 'Broadway' CPU at 729 MHz and a 'Hollywood' GPU — closely related to the GameCube's chips, just faster. Cheap, cool, quiet, and deliberately not chasing HD. Nintendo bet on input, not graphics.

The Wii Remote
the idea

A wand with accelerometers and an infrared pointer (reading a sensor bar) let players swing, point, and gesture. It pulled in people who'd never touched a controller — the real engine of the Wii's enormous sales.

Backward and Frugal
the rest

Full GameCube backward compatibility, SD-card storage, and tiny power draw made it approachable and affordable — a console designed around accessibility over spec-sheet bragging rights.
